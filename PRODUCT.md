# El Nopalito Taqueria — Product Context

## Register

**Brand.** This is a marketing site for a small, new family taqueria. The site IS the product — it exists to drive phone calls, directions clicks, and foot traffic. There is no app, no online ordering, no account.

## Users & Purpose

**Primary:**
- Hungry locals in Oak Cliff, Kiestwood Village, southwest Dallas, Duncanville, DeSoto searching "tacos near me," "tacos Oak Cliff," "best tacos in Dallas."
- Friends/family of customers receiving a recommendation and looking up the spot before driving.
- Tuesday lunch crowd specifically looking for taco deals.

**Job to be done:** Decide in 5–10 seconds whether this is worth the drive, then either tap to call or tap to navigate. They are NOT browsing. They want: where it is, what's good, can I trust it.

**Anti-job:** This is not a place people browse for inspiration. We are not Yelp, Eater, or a foodie blog. We need to feel real, local, tasty — not curated.

## Brand Personality

- **Warm, family-run, hidden-gem.** A place an in-the-know neighbor sends you to.
- **Confident but humble.** "Quietly the best in Oak Cliff." Not loud. Not corporate.
- **Hungry-energy.** Photos do most of the selling.
- **Small menu, big confidence.** Tacos / quesatacos / birria / quesadillas. We do four things excellently.
- Voice: direct, casual, punchy. No corporate restaurant tone. No "exquisite culinary journey." Talk like a real person who works in a taqueria.

## Anti-References

- **Chipotle / generic chain Mexican.** Sterile, scaled, drive-thru energy.
- **Olive Garden / TGI-Fridays restaurant template.** Stock-photo families laughing.
- **Yelp listing aesthetic.** Lists, badges, clutter.
- **Tourist-trap Mexican kitsch.** Sombrero clip-art, cartoon mariachis, cliché serape stripes used badly.
- **Marketing-agency restaurant landing.** Big stock photo + "Authentic Cuisine" in a script font + a 30-item dropdown nav.

## Inspirations

- **taquerialaventana.com** — full-bleed food photography, scrapbook/polaroid feel with washi tape, scrolling marquee, warm Mexico City street-food energy.
- **Bishop Arts Dallas mom-and-pop aesthetic** — handwritten chalkboard energy, real photos, neighborhood pride.

## Strategic Design Principles

1. **Photo > copy.** The taco shot does more work than any headline. Hero is full-bleed.
2. **Two CTAs win:** Call & Directions. Everything else is secondary. They never compete with these.
3. **Taco Tuesday $1.99 is the hook.** Surface it everywhere — it's the reason a stranger pulls up the first time.
4. **NAP consistency** (Name, Address, Phone) on every page is non-negotiable for local SEO.
5. **No false sophistication.** No glassmorphism, no script fonts, no gradient text, no AI-slop palettes. Real warmth, real food, real signage feel.
6. **Mobile-first.** 80%+ of "tacos near me" searches are on phones in cars. Sticky bottom CTA must always be reachable.
7. **Crave-induce.** The visceral test: does looking at the homepage make you hungry? If no, ship a better photo.

## Accessibility

- WCAG AA contrast minimum. Cream-on-ink and white-on-primary need explicit checking.
- Hours, phone, address must be readable text (not in images) for screen readers and indexing.
- Sticky mobile CTA bar must not obstruct content.

## Constraints

- **Stack:** Next.js 16 (App Router) + Tailwind v4 + Vercel.
- **Photos:** 4 real photos shot by owner — storefront, taco-plate, inside-taco, extra (exterior). No stock photos ever.
- **Copy:** Owner is hands-off; site copy is written by the developer, must read like the owner could plausibly say it.
